# Texas Contractor License Requirements

## TDLR (Texas Department of Licensing & Regulation)
Primary licensing authority for most trades in Texas.
Website: tdlr.texas.gov
License lookup: tdlr.texas.gov/LicenseSearch

### Trades Licensed by TDLR
- **HVAC** — Air Conditioning & Refrigeration Contractor License required
  - Must be licensed as ACR contractor to perform HVAC work for hire
  - Technician EPA 608 certification also required
  - Background check required
  
- **Electrical** — Electrical Contractor License required
  - Must employ licensed Master Electrician
  - Individual electricians: Apprentice → Journeyman → Master licensing path
  
- **Mold Remediation** — Mold Remediation Contractor License
  - Required for remediation work over $1,500
  
- **Irrigation** — Licensed Irrigator required (separate from TCEQ)
  
- **Water Well** — Water Well Driller License

### TSBPE (Texas State Board of Plumbing Examiners)
Website: tsbpe.texas.gov
- **Plumbing** — Plumbing Contractor/Master Plumber required
- Apprentice → Journeyman → Master licensing path
- Separate from TDLR

### No License Required (Texas) for:
- Roofing (no state license required, but cities may require permits)
- General painting
- Landscaping/lawn care
- Pressure washing
- Fence installation
- Drywall
- Flooring
- Handyman work under $5,000 per project (county-dependent)

**NOTE:** Many Texas cities (Dallas, Fort Worth, Plano, etc.) require local permits for roofing, electrical, plumbing, and HVAC work regardless of state licensing.

## ProLnk License Verification Process
1. Partner uploads license document (PDF/image) to Briefcase
2. System OCR extracts license number and expiry date
3. Admin verifies against TDLR database (manual or via Puppeteer scraper)
4. License status updated in Briefcase
5. Compliance agent monitors expiry dates quarterly

## Clearance Levels Requiring Additional Vetting
- **School/Government**: Enhanced background check + OSHA certification typically required
- **Healthcare**: May require additional certifications and drug screening
- **Commercial**: Varies by facility; Briefcase + Pro Pass reviewed by facility

## ProLnk Scout — Legal Note
ProLnk Home Assessments are "Property Documentation Services" not licensed home inspections (TREC-regulated). Scouts document visible conditions; AI generates analysis. Scouts do not certify structural integrity or sign off on system functionality. This distinction must be maintained to avoid TREC licensing requirements.

TREC (Texas Real Estate Commission) licenses home inspectors for paid property inspections. If Scout program evolves to offer certifiable inspections, licensed inspectors must be involved.
