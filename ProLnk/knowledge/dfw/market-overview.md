# DFW Market Overview for ProLnk

## Launch Market: Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex

### Key Cities and Target Areas
- **Dallas** (75201-75254) — Urban core, high-density
- **Plano** (75023-75093) — Affluent suburb, high homeowner income
- **Frisco** (75033-75036) — Fastest growing, new construction + aging inventory
- **Allen** (75002-75013) — Strong middle market
- **McKinney** (75069-75072) — Rapidly growing, mix of ages
- **Prosper** (75078) — Premium suburb, high home values
- **Celina** (75009) — Fast growth, new construction
- **The Colony** (75056) — Lake access, good homeowner density
- **Garland** (75040-75044) — Working class, high service need
- **Mesquite** (75149-75150) — Similar to Garland
- **Irving** (75038-75063) — Commercial heavy, some residential
- **Arlington** (76001-76017) — Major market, diverse
- **Fort Worth** (76101-76179) — Large market, second priority

### DFW Housing Statistics (2024-2025)
- ~2.7 million homes in the DFW metroplex
- Average home value: ~$350,000 (varies widely by area)
- Average age of housing stock: ~25-30 years
- High storm activity: hail season April-June, tornado season spring/fall

### Most In-Demand Trades in DFW
1. **HVAC** — Critical in Texas climate (extreme heat), avg job $3,000-$8,000
2. **Roofing** — Hail damage from storms, avg job $8,000-$25,000+
3. **Pest control** — Year-round, avg $500-$2,000/year
4. **Lawn care** — Weekly/bi-weekly, avg $80-$200/service
5. **Plumbing** — High demand, avg $500-$3,000
6. **Foundation repair** — Unique to Texas clay soil, avg $5,000-$25,000
7. **Tree service** — After storms especially, avg $800-$3,000
8. **Water mitigation** — After storm/flooding events, avg $3,000-$15,000
9. **Electrical** — Panel upgrades popular, avg $2,000-$6,000
10. **Fencing** — Wind damage, avg $2,000-$8,000

### DFW Storm Calendar
- **April-June**: Peak hail season (50+ hail events/year in DFW)
- **March-May**: Tornado season
- **June-September**: Hurricane remnants (tropical moisture)
- **December-February**: Ice storms, frozen pipes

### Competitor Landscape in DFW
- **Angi/HomeAdvisor**: Dominant in lead selling but contractors hate it
- **Thumbtack**: Consumer facing, contractor frustration high
- **Yelp**: Review focused, not lead generation
- **Nextdoor**: Word of mouth, not structured
- **Local Facebook groups**: Active, no vetting
- **ProLnk's position**: Only platform where contractors generate leads from their own work
