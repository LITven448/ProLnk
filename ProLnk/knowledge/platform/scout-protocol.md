# ProLnk Scout Assessment Protocol

## What is a Scout?
A ProLnk Scout is a certified property documentation specialist trained to conduct whole-home assessments across 12 zones. Scouts are NOT licensed home inspectors — they provide a "Property Documentation Service" where they capture photos and observations, and AI generates the condition analysis.

## Scout Certification Levels
- **Scout Basic** — Residential assessments only
- **Scout Advanced** — Residential + commercial up to 10,000 sq ft
- **Scout Commercial** — All property types including multifamily, schools, healthcare

## The 12 Assessment Zones

### Zone 1: Roof & Gutters
- Photograph from ground (multiple angles)
- Note visible: shingle condition, moss/algae, flashing, ridge cap, gutters, downspouts
- Look for: missing shingles, granule loss, sagging, debris accumulation
- Use drone photo if available (not required)

### Zone 2: Exterior / Siding / Foundation
- Walk perimeter, photograph each side
- Note: siding material, cracks, gaps, paint condition, foundation visibility
- Look for: efflorescence, settling cracks, moisture staining, rot, damaged trim

### Zone 3: Windows & Doors
- Photograph each exterior window and door
- Note: seal integrity (fogging = failed seal), condition, weatherstripping
- Check: deadbolts, storm windows, screens

### Zone 4: HVAC Systems
- Photograph: outdoor unit(s), thermostat, air handler if accessible
- Record: manufacturer nameplate (brand, model, serial, year)
- Note: filter condition, refrigerant lines, condenser condition, drainage
- Age estimate: from serial number (first 4 digits usually MMYY or YYMM depending on brand)

### Zone 5: Electrical
- Photograph: main panel, any sub-panels
- Note: panel brand, amperage (stamped on main breaker), double-tapped breakers, GFCI locations
- Flag: Federal Pacific/Stab-Lok panels (fire hazard), aluminum wiring, knob-and-tube
- DO NOT perform load tests or touch wiring

### Zone 6: Plumbing
- Photograph: water heater (nameplate), visible pipes under sinks, water pressure gauge if accessible
- Note: water heater age/brand/BTU, pipe material (copper/PVC/galvanized/CPVC)
- Look for: corrosion, active leaks, water staining, mineral deposits
- Test: faucet pressure, water heater temperature setting

### Zone 7: Appliances
- Photograph each major appliance (refrigerator, stove, dishwasher, washer/dryer)
- Record: brand, model, approximate age (from sticker or visible wear)
- Note: condition, any obvious issues
- DO NOT disassemble or perform diagnostic tests

### Zone 8: Interior Rooms
- Photograph: each room (2 photos minimum — wide angle + ceiling/floor detail)
- Note: flooring material and condition, wall condition, ceiling condition, fixtures
- Look for: water staining, mold, cracking drywall, outdated fixtures

### Zone 9: Attic (if accessible)
- Photograph: insulation, decking, ventilation
- Note: insulation type and estimated depth (R-value), moisture stains, ventilation adequacy
- Look for: vermiculite, damaged decking, pest activity

### Zone 10: Crawl Space / Basement (if applicable)
- Photograph: moisture barrier, insulation, visible structural members
- Note: moisture level (estimated), drainage, pest activity
- DO NOT enter if structural integrity is questionable

### Zone 11: Garage
- Photograph: garage door, opener, internal walls, electrical panel (if separate)
- Note: door age/condition, opener brand/age, any storage of hazardous materials

### Zone 12: Exterior Property
- Photograph: driveway, walkways, deck/patio, fence, pool (if present), landscaping
- Note: concrete/asphalt condition, fence condition, pool equipment age, tree proximity to structures
- Look for: trip hazards, drainage issues, overgrown vegetation touching structure

## Photo Standards
- Minimum: 2 photos per zone (more for large zones)
- Photo type: Wide angle first, then detail shots
- Lighting: Turn on lights for interior shots
- Label: Before = arriving at the zone, After = not applicable for assessments (this is documentation only)
- GPS: iPhone captures GPS automatically — ensure Location Services is ON in camera app

## What Scouts Do NOT Do
- Do not certify any system as "functional" or "safe"
- Do not provide professional engineering opinions
- Do not turn off utilities or disconnect systems
- Do not climb on the roof (photograph from ground unless already at roof level for a job)
- Do not enter confined spaces without proper safety equipment

## Scout Commission Structure
1. **Assessment fee** — set by Scout, paid directly by homeowner ($150-$2,000 depending on property type)
2. **Origination commission** — Scout is locked as sourcePartnerId on all opportunities from the report; earns referral commission when jobs close
3. **GC/PM fee** (optional) — 8-12% of total project value if Scout project-manages the work

## Homeowner Consent
Before beginning an assessment:
- Scout must obtain verbal consent from property owner or authorized agent
- Document: "Homeowner/agent [name] has authorized this documentation assessment on [date]"
- Record consent in the Scout app
- Scouts are documenting, not inspecting — no TREC disclosure required
