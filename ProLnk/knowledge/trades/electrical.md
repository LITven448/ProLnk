# Electrical — Trade Knowledge

## What Electrical covers
All residential and commercial electrical systems: panels, wiring, outlets, switches, lighting, EV chargers, generators, smart home systems, ceiling fans.

- **Panel inspection**: $150-300

- **Panel upgrade 200A→400A** (large homes, EV needs): $3,000-6,000
- **GFCI outlet installation**: $150-250 per outlet
- **Outlet/switch replacement**: $75-150 per unit
- **Ceiling fan installation**: $150-300
- **Light fixture replacement**: $100-250
- **EV charger installation (Level 2)**: $500-1,500

## High-Priority Detection Signals

### Federal Pacific / Stab-Lok Panels (CRITICAL)
- Brand name on panel: "Federal Pacific" or "FPE"
- Breakers labeled "Stab-Lok"
- Known fire hazard — breakers don't trip properly
- Replacement priority: IMMEDIATE — insurance may require it
- Estimated replacement: $1,500-3,000

### Zinsco Panels (HIGH RISK)
- Teal/turquoise breakers, "Zinsco" or "Sylvania" label
- Similar issues to Federal Pacific
- Replacement priority: IMMEDIATE

### Aluminum Wiring (CONCERN)
- Visible in attic or older homes (pre-1973)
- Silvery colored wiring (copper is orange-red)
- Requires COPALUM crimp connectors or pigtailing
- Risk: loose connections, overheating

### Double-Tapped Breakers
- Two wires connected to a single breaker (visible in panel)
- Code violation unless breaker is rated for it
- Fix: add a breaker or subpanel

### Age Signals
- **60A service** (one main breaker): 1950s-1960s homes, insufficient for modern use
- **100A service**: 1960s-1980s, adequate for basic homes
- **200A service**: current standard for single family
- **Older panel brands to flag**: Pushmatic, Bulldog

## Texas Licensing
- Electrical Contractor License from TDLR required
- Must employ or be a licensed Master Electrician
- Individual license path: Apprentice → Journeyman (4 years exp + exam) → Master (2 more years + exam)
- Permits required for most work in DFW cities

## Opportunity Detection in Photos
- Panel brand visible as Federal Pacific, Zinsco, Pushmatic, Bulldog
- Panel looks old (60A or 100A service → upgrade opportunity)
- No GFCI outlets visible in bathroom/kitchen (code violation)
- Knob-and-tube wiring visible in attic
- Extension cords used as permanent wiring (hazard)
- Ungrounded 2-prong outlets in older home
- No arc-fault protection (AFCI breakers) in bedrooms
