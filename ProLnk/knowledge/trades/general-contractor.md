# General Contractor — Trade Knowledge

## What a GC Does
A General Contractor (GC) manages entire construction projects, coordinating multiple trade subcontractors. They're the point of contact for complex multi-trade projects.

## When to Route to GC vs. Trade Specialist
- **Single trade job** (just HVAC, just plumbing): Route to trade specialist
- **Multi-trade project** (kitchen remodel needing plumbing + electrical + carpentry): Route to GC
- **Large value project** (>$25,000): Consider GC for project management
- **Scout assessment projects**: GC bid through Bid Board

## Common GC Jobs in DFW
- **Full kitchen remodel**: $25,000-80,000+
- **Bathroom remodel**: $8,000-30,000+
- **Room addition**: $40,000-120,000+
- **Whole home renovation**: $100,000-500,000+
- **Garage conversion to living space**: $20,000-50,000
- **Accessory dwelling unit (ADU)**: $80,000-200,000+
- **Deck/patio build**: $8,000-30,000+
- **Pool construction**: $40,000-100,000+

## Texas GC Licensing
- **No state license required** for general contractors in Texas
- BUT most cities (Dallas, Plano, Frisco, etc.) require local permits for structural work
- **Residential contractor registration** — some cities require it
- Many GCs voluntarily pursue NASCLA accreditation or NARI membership

## ProLnk GC Category
For ProLnk routing purposes, GC is a catch-all for:
- Full renovations
- Multi-trade projects
- Projects where a Scout assessment generated many findings across categories
- Projects on the Bid Board that need a single point of responsibility

## Bid Board Usage
When a Scout creates a whole-home assessment and the project is large/complex:
- Option A: Post individual trade line items to Bid Board (each trade bids separately)
- Option B: Post as single GC project (one GC manages all trades)
- Option C: Scout acts as PM (Scout earns GC management fee)

## Detection Signals Routing to GC
Any of these combinations:
- 5+ different trades needed from a single property
- Single estimate > $25,000
- "Full renovation" or "remodel" mentioned in job notes
- Commercial or multifamily property with complex scope
