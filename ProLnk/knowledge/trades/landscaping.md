# Landscaping & Lawn Care — Trade Knowledge

## Services Covered
Lawn mowing, landscaping design/install, irrigation, hardscaping, drainage, mulching, fertilization, tree care, outdoor lighting, sod installation.

## Common Services & Pricing (DFW)
- **Lawn mowing (standard lot)**: $45-120/visit
- **Seasonal cleanup**: $200-600
- **Mulching (per yard installed)**: $50-80
- **Sod installation (per sq ft)**: $1-2
- **Landscape design + install**: $5,000-30,000+
- **Irrigation system install**: $3,000-7,000
- **Irrigation repair**: $150-500
- **Sprinkler head replacement**: $65-150
- **French drain**: $2,000-8,000
- **Retaining wall**: $5-15/sq ft labor + materials
- **Outdoor lighting install**: $2,000-8,000
- **Tree trimming**: $300-800 per tree
- **Tree removal (small-medium)**: $400-1,500
- **Tree removal (large)**: $1,500-5,000+
- **Stump grinding**: $100-400

## DFW-Specific Landscaping
- **Bermuda grass**: Most common warm-season grass in DFW. Goes dormant (brown) in winter — normal.
- **St. Augustine**: Common in shadier areas. Prone to chinch bugs and brown patch.
- **Zoysia**: Increasingly popular. Slower growing, more drought-resistant.
- **Buffalo grass**: Native, drought-tolerant, lower maintenance.
- **Watering restrictions**: Many DFW cities have Stage 1-3 watering restrictions in summer. Important for irrigation systems.

## Detection Signals in Photos
- **Overgrown lawn** (routine — lawn care opportunity)
- **Dead/patchy grass** (routine — sod, overseeding, soil treatment)
- **Visible irrigation heads** (check for broken, missing, overspray — repair opportunity)
- **No mulch or old mulch** in beds (routine — mulching opportunity)
- **Drainage issues** (standing water visible, erosion, washout — drainage opportunity)
- **Overgrown trees** touching house or power lines (IMMEDIATE — fire/safety hazard)
- **Dead trees** (IMMEDIATE — fall risk)
- **Cracked/settled concrete** walkways or driveway edges (hardscaping)
- **Invasive species** visible (routine removal)

## License Note
- No state license required for most landscaping in Texas
- Irrigation contractors: Licensed Irrigator required for new irrigation system installation (Texas Water Development Board)
- Tree arborists: ISA Certified Arborist is a quality signal but not legally required
- Pesticide application in landscaping context: May require pesticide applicator license
