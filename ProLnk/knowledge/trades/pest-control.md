# Pest Control — Trade Knowledge

## Why Pest Control Is Huge in DFW
Texas climate supports year-round pest activity. DFW has significant:
- Fire ant populations (stinging hazard, damages lawn/landscaping)
- Termite activity (subterranean and Formosan termites)
- Cockroach populations (German and American)
- Mosquito populations (creek and pond areas)
- Rodents (mice, rats — especially in fall as temperatures drop)

## Common Pest Control Services
- **Quarterly general pest control**: $100-200/quarter ($400-800/year)
- **Termite inspection**: $75-200
- **Termite treatment (liquid)**: $1,000-3,000+
- **Termite treatment (Sentricon bait)**: $800-1,500 initial + $300-500/year monitoring
- **Bed bug treatment**: $1,500-4,000
- **Mosquito treatment/prevention**: $500-2,000/season
- **Rodent exclusion**: $500-2,000
- **Wildlife removal (raccoon, squirrel)**: $300-800

## Termite Detection (HIGH VALUE)
Termites cause $5B+ in damage annually in the US. Texas is high-risk.

### Signs of Termite Activity in Photos
- **Mud tubes** on foundation or exterior walls — visible brown tubes ≈ pencil-width
- **Damaged wood** (bubbling paint, hollow sound, soft spots)
- **Swarmers** (winged termites) near windows or light fixtures
- **Frass** (termite droppings — looks like sawdust)

If ANY of these are detected: flag `urgency: "immediate"` and `isInsuranceClaim: false` (termites not covered by standard homeowners insurance).

## Seasonal Timing
- **Spring** (March-May): Termite swarm season, fire ant season, mosquito season begins
- **Summer**: Mosquitoes peak, cockroach activity high
- **Fall** (Oct-Nov): Rodents seeking warmth, brown recluse active
- **Winter**: Mostly indoor pests (German cockroaches, rodents), termites still active underground

## Detection Signs to Flag
- Mud tubes on foundation (termite — IMMEDIATE)
- Wood damage visible on siding/trim/decking (could be termite or carpenter ant)
- Ant mounds in yard (fire ants — routine)
- Rodent droppings visible near entry points (routine to immediate depending on severity)
- Wasp/hornet nests under eaves (immediate — safety)
- Mosquito breeding areas (standing water visible in yard — routine)

## No State License Required
Texas does require a Structural Pest Control license for pesticide application (TPCL from TPCL).
The Structural Pest Control Service (SPCS) at Texas Department of Agriculture licenses pest control companies.
Partners doing pest control need a SPCS license — flag this in Briefcase requirements.
