## Texas-Specific Plumbing
- **Hard water**: DFW has extremely hard water (300-700 PPM). Water softeners very common.
- **Freeze events**: Texas ice storms (2021 freeze was severe) cause burst pipes. PEX is more freeze-resistant than copper.
- **Slab construction**: Most DFW homes are slab-on-grade. Slab leaks are a significant issue — require tunneling or rerouting pipes.
- **PEX adoption**: Post-2000 homes increasingly use PEX (flexible, more freeze-resistant than copper)

## Pipe Materials by Age
- **1920s-1960s**: Galvanized steel — corrodes, reduces water pressure, orange/brown water
- **1960s-1990s**: Copper — good, but can corrode in acidic water
- **1980s-2000s**: CPVC — brittle over time, prone to cracking
- **1990s-present**: PEX — flexible, freeze-resistant, preferred modern choice
- **Polybutylene (gray plastic)**: 1978-1995 — class action recall, failure-prone, HIGH PRIORITY REPLACEMENT

## Water Heater Age & Replacement
- **Tank water heater lifespan**: 8-12 years
- **Age from serial number**: Most manufacturers encode year in first 4 digits (varies by brand)
  - Rheem/Ruud: 2nd digit = decade, 3rd-4th = year within decade (e.g., "F073" = 2007)
  - AO Smith: 1st digit = decade letter (A=1994, B=1995...), 2nd = year, 3rd-4th = week
- **Tankless lifespan**: 20+ years with maintenance
- **Signs of end-of-life**: Rust-colored water, rumbling/popping noises, leaking from base, insufficient hot water

## Opportunity Detection in Photos
- Water heater label showing age 10+ years
- Galvanized pipes visible (dark gray, often corroded)
- Polybutylene pipes (gray plastic pipes — URGENT)
- Water staining on walls/ceilings (active or past leak)
- Low water pressure visible (dripping faucets, slow fill)
- Corroded shutoff valves
- Missing pressure relief valve (safety issue)
- Old toilet (pre-1994 uses 3.5+ gallons/flush vs. 1.28 gpf modern)

## Texas License Requirement
- Master Plumber license from TSBPE (Texas State Board of Plumbing Examiners)
- Plumbing contractor must employ or be a licensed master plumber
- Permits required for most work
- Inspections required for new installs and major repairs
