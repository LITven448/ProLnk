# Tree Service — Trade Knowledge

## Common Tree Services
- **Tree trimming (small tree < 25ft)**: $300-700
- **Tree trimming (medium 25-60ft)**: $700-1,500
- **Tree trimming (large >60ft)**: $1,500-3,000+
- **Tree removal (small)**: $400-900
- **Tree removal (medium)**: $900-2,000
- **Tree removal (large/hazardous)**: $2,000-6,000+
- **Stump grinding**: $100-400 per stump
- **Emergency storm removal**: $1,000-10,000+ (hazard premium)
- **Deep root fertilization**: $200-600
- **Cabling/bracing**: $500-1,500
- **Disease/pest treatment**: $300-800

## DFW Storm Damage (Highest Value)
After hail, high wind, tornado events:
- Trees uprooted or blocking driveways/structures
- Large limbs snapped and fallen on roof, fence, vehicles
- Trees leaning significantly after root disturbance

**Emergency removals after storms command premium pricing and are often insurance claims.**

## Texas Tree Species (Common)
- **Live oak** (evergreen, drops leaves in spring) — large, dense, high removal cost
- **Post oak / Blackjack oak** — common in DFW, moderate removal
- **Cedar/Juniper** — extremely common, fast-growing, some hate them (allergies)
- **Bradford/Callery Pear** — invasive, splits in storms, should be removed
- **Crepe myrtle** — smaller ornamental, frequent over-trimming ("crepe murder")
- **Pecan** — large, valuable, nut-bearing, high removal cost

## Detection Signals in Photos

### IMMEDIATE (Safety / Damage Risk)
- **Tree limb on or touching roof** — weight, moisture damage risk
- **Large limb hanging (widow-maker)** — fall risk
- **Tree leaning significantly** toward structure — fall risk
- **Tree touching power lines** — UTILITY EMERGENCY (don't trim yourself, call utility)
- **Split trunk with major limbs** — imminent failure risk
- **Uprooted tree** partially or fully down

### ROUTINE
- **Dead branches** visible (brown leaves in summer = dead)
- **Significant overhang** over roof (ongoing debris, moss, moisture)
- **Root proximity to foundation** or plumbing lines
- **Diseased tree** (spotted leaves, bracket fungi on trunk)
- **Overgrown** touching fence/neighbor's property

## ISA Certified Arborist
Professional credential from International Society of Arboriculture.
Not legally required but a strong trust signal. Recommend looking for ISA certs for larger jobs.

## Insurance Claims
Storm tree damage: Covered if the tree falls and damages a covered structure (house, fence).
Cost of removing the tree itself from the yard (not on structure): usually NOT covered unless it's blocking driveway per policy terms.

**If tree fell on roof or structure → flag `isInsuranceClaim: true`**
**If tree just fell in yard → flag `isInsuranceClaim: false`**
