# Water Mitigation & Restoration — Trade Knowledge

## What Water Mitigation Covers
Emergency water damage extraction, drying, mold remediation, and structural restoration after flooding, burst pipes, roof leaks, or appliance failures.

## Common Services
- **Emergency water extraction**: $500-1,500
- **Full drying/remediation (standard room)**: $1,500-5,000
- **Mold remediation**: $1,500-15,000+
- **Full restoration (major flood)**: $10,000-50,000+
- **Insurance-funded restoration**: often $15,000-80,000+

## Insurance Claim Potential (HIGHEST VALUE)
Water damage from sudden events is almost always covered by homeowners insurance:
- Burst pipe = COVERED
- Appliance failure (washing machine, water heater) = COVERED
- Storm flooding = may be covered (depends on policy, flood insurance separate)
- Roof leak → interior damage = COVERED
- Gradual leak (maintenance issue) = NOT COVERED

**Always flag water damage as potential insurance claim** — these are the highest-value jobs in the platform.

## Detection Signals in Photos

### IMMEDIATE — Active or Recent Damage
- **Water stains on ceiling** (circular brown rings = past or ongoing leak)
- **Wet or warped flooring** (wood buckling, carpet dark)
- **Visible mold** (black, green, or white fuzzy growth on any surface)
- **Peeling paint/wallpaper** (moisture behind wall)
- **Efflorescence** on basement/foundation walls (mineral deposits from water)
- **Swollen door frames** (moisture swelling wood)
- **Damaged drywall** (soft, crumbling, paper face peeling)

### Storm-Related (flag isInsuranceClaim: true)
- **Ceiling stains after recent storm** = storm damage → insurance claim
- **Wet crawlspace** after heavy rain = flooding
- **Basement water** after storm = flooding

## The Mitigation Process
1. **Extract** standing water (truck-mounted or portable extractors)
2. **Dry** with industrial dehumidifiers + air movers (3-5 days minimum)
3. **Monitor** moisture levels daily with meters
4. **Remediate** any mold found
5. **Restore** damaged materials (drywall, flooring, insulation)

## DFW-Specific
- Hail storms frequently damage roofs → interior water intrusion
- Ice Storm 2021: massive pipe burst events across DFW → peak demand for mitigation
- Flash flooding common in low-lying areas
- Crawlspace moisture issues prevalent due to clay soils and poor drainage

## License Requirement
- Mold remediation requires a TDLR Mold Remediation Contractor License (MOLD-R)
- General water mitigation/drying does not require a specific license
- Most full-service restoration companies hold both
