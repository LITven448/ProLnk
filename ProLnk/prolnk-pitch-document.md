ProLnk is the trust infrastructure layer for the $842 billion home services industry — a verified professional network that routes homeowners to the right contractor at the right moment, while generating proprietary property intelligence data that is worth more than the referral fees.

## Part I: The Problem

### For the Homeowner: The Trust Vacuum

Hiring a contractor in America is broken. There are approximately **10.5 million licensed contractors** in the United States, but the homeowner has almost no reliable way to know which ones are actually trustworthy before they show up at the door.

The data on this is damning. **1 in 10 Americans has experienced a contractor scam** [1]. A 2025 Leaf Home survey found that **nearly 70% of homeowners worry about unreliable contractors**, and **41% report being deceived by a service provider** [2]. A 2024 CNBC survey found that **33% of homeowners would hire a contractor with a questionable reputation just to save money** — not because they want to, but because they have no better signal to distinguish good from bad [3].

The existing "solutions" have made this worse, not better. Angi (formerly HomeAdvisor) and Thumbtack sell the same lead to 4-6 contractors simultaneously. The homeowner gets bombarded with calls from strangers. The contractor who calls first wins — not the one who is most qualified. The homeowner is left to sort through competing bids from people they know nothing about, hoping for the best.

Google reviews are gamed. Yelp is gamed. Nextdoor recommendations are anecdotal. The BBB is a pay-to-play membership organization. None of these systems produce a verified, structured, multi-signal trust score that a homeowner can actually rely on.

The result: homeowners either overpay out of fear, underpay and get burned, or delay repairs until small problems become expensive emergencies. **85% of American homeowners faced a home repair challenge in the past year** [4], and the average homeowner now spends **$8,800 annually on home maintenance** [5] — most of it spent in a trust vacuum.

### For the Service Professional: The CAC Crisis

The problem on the contractor side is a mirror image. Good contractors — licensed, insured, experienced, honest — are drowning in customer acquisition costs while being commoditized by platforms that treat them as interchangeable.

The numbers are brutal. The average cost to acquire a new customer through Angi is **$542 per booked job** [6]. Google Local Services Ads run **$168 per booked job** on the low end. For roofing, HVAC, and remodeling, CAC can reach **$600-$1,200 per new customer** depending on the channel.

A roofing contractor with a $12,000 average job size and a $900 CAC is spending 7.5% of revenue just to find the customer — before labor, materials, overhead, or profit. A plumber with a $450 average job and a $90 CAC is spending 20% of revenue on acquisition. These are not sustainable economics, and they get worse every year as the platforms raise prices and the market gets more crowded.

The deeper problem is that the current lead generation model is fundamentally adversarial. Angi and HomeAdvisor make money when contractors buy leads, regardless of whether those leads convert. They have no financial incentive to send better leads — only more leads. The contractor pays whether the lead answers the phone or not. This misalignment has created an industry where contractors spend enormous amounts of money on leads they can't close, while simultaneously being commoditized against 5 competitors bidding on the same job.

Good contractors — the ones who do great work, maintain licenses, carry insurance, and build real customer relationships — have no structural advantage in this system. They compete on the same terms as the fly-by-night operators. The market cannot distinguish between them.

### For the Data Buyer: The Property Intelligence Gap

This is the third problem, and it is the one that most people miss entirely.

Every home repair, every maintenance visit, every upgrade creates a data point about the condition of a property. When a roofer replaces a 25-year-old roof, that is a material fact about the property's risk profile. When an HVAC contractor notes that a system is 18 years old and running inefficiently, that is a fact that an insurance underwriter, a mortgage lender, a home warranty company, and a prospective buyer all want to know.

This data exists. It is generated every day by millions of service visits. But it is trapped in the heads of individual contractors, in handwritten invoices, in disconnected field service management apps that do not talk to each other. No one has built the infrastructure to capture it, verify it, and make it available at scale.

The companies that buy this data — Verisk ($3.07 billion in 2025 revenue [7]), CoreLogic, ATTOM Data, insurance carriers, mortgage servicers — are currently paying for proxies and estimates. They use satellite imagery, permit records, and actuarial models to guess at property conditions. They would pay a significant premium for **verified, ground-truth data from licensed professionals who actually visited the property**.

The property intelligence software market alone is valued at **$5 billion in 2025** [8]. The insurance analytics market that relies on property data is orders of magnitude larger. ProLnk, by building the verified professional network and capturing structured data from every service visit, is positioned to become a primary data supplier to this entire ecosystem.

## Part III: The Solution

### What ProLnk Actually Is

ProLnk is not a lead generation platform. It is not a marketplace. It is not a directory.

**ProLnk is a verified professional network with an AI-powered routing engine and a proprietary property intelligence layer.**

The distinction matters. Lead generation platforms (Angi, Thumbtack) sell contact information. ProLnk sells verified trust. The difference in value proposition — and in defensibility — is enormous.

The three-layer architecture:

**Layer 1: The Verification Engine (TrustedPro Score)**
Every service professional on the platform passes a 7-checkpoint verification process: business license, liability insurance, background check, business registration, references, portfolio, and identity. Each checkpoint is weighted and produces a Trust Score (0-100) and a badge level (Bronze through Platinum). This is not self-reported data — it is admin-verified. The result is a network where every professional has been vetted to a standard that no existing platform matches.

**Layer 2: The Priority Score Engine (PPS)**
Once verified, professionals are ranked by an 8-signal performance score that updates in real time: tier commitment, lead close rate, acceptance rate, job documentation, customer reviews, network referrals, and response speed. Higher scores mean first access to new leads. This creates a meritocratic system where the best performers are structurally rewarded — the opposite of the current market where speed-to-call is the only differentiator.

**Layer 3: The Property Intelligence Layer (TrustyPro)**
The homeowner-facing brand, TrustyPro, captures structured data at the point of service. Homeowners upload photos; AI analyzes them and identifies repair needs, upgrade opportunities, and insurance-relevant events. Every service visit is documented with before/after photos, job type, contractor credentials, and outcome. Over time, this builds a **Home Health Vault** — a verified maintenance record for every property in the network.

This data, aggregated and anonymized, is the long-term asset. It is the thing that makes ProLnk defensible against well-funded competitors. Angi can outspend ProLnk on marketing. They cannot replicate five years of verified, ground-truth property condition data.

## Part IV: Value Proposition by Audience

### For the Homeowner

**The pitch:** *"Know exactly who is coming to your home before they arrive — and why you can trust them."*

The homeowner value proposition has three components:

First, **verified trust at the point of hire**. Every ProLnk professional has passed the same 7-checkpoint verification that a major employer would require. The homeowner sees a Trust Score, a badge level, and a performance history before making any decision. This is not a star rating from anonymous strangers — it is a structured, admin-verified credential.

Second, **AI-powered problem identification**. TrustyPro's photo analysis engine identifies repair needs, upgrade opportunities, and insurance-relevant damage from photos the homeowner takes on their phone. The homeowner does not need to know what is wrong with their house — the AI tells them, routes them to the right professional, and gives them an estimated cost range before anyone shows up.

Third, **the Home Health Vault**. Every repair, every maintenance visit, every upgrade is documented and stored. When the homeowner sells the house, they have a verified maintenance record that is worth real money — studies show that documented maintenance history increases sale price and reduces time on market. When they file an insurance claim, they have photographic evidence. When they wonder if the HVAC is due for replacement, the system tells them.

The homeowner pays nothing. The platform is free to use. The economics work because the professional pays a commission only when a job is completed — and only when ProLnk was the source of the introduction.

### For the Service Professional

**The pitch:** *"Stop paying for leads you can't close. Pay only when you win — and win more often because homeowners already trust you."*

The service professional value proposition is a direct attack on the CAC crisis described above.

ProLnk's commission-only model means the professional pays nothing until a job is completed. There is no monthly subscription fee for leads, no pay-per-click, no paying for leads that don't answer the phone. The commission rate is set below the industry CAC for every trade category — so the professional is always paying less than they would on any alternative platform.

The comparison is stark. An HVAC contractor on Angi pays $542 per booked job, with no guarantee of quality. On ProLnk, they pay a commission only on completed jobs, at a rate that is 30-50% below their current CAC. The math is simple: ProLnk is cheaper, the leads are warmer (homeowner already identified the problem via AI scan), and the professional's verified credentials are displayed to the homeowner before the first call.

Beyond economics, ProLnk offers something no other platform does: **a meritocratic ranking system that rewards quality**. The best professionals — the ones who close jobs, respond quickly, document their work, and earn reviews — rise to the top of the routing queue. For the first time, being a great contractor is a structural competitive advantage, not just a nice-to-have.

The network effect compounds this. A professional who refers another professional earns points in the Priority Score. A professional who documents jobs with photos earns points. Every action that makes the network better makes the individual professional's position in the network better. This is a flywheel, not a treadmill.

### For the Data Buyer

**The pitch:** *"Ground-truth property condition data from verified professionals — the data set that doesn't exist anywhere else."*

The data buyer audience includes insurance carriers, mortgage servicers, home warranty companies, real estate investors, municipal governments, and property analytics firms. What they all share is a need for accurate, current, verified information about the condition of individual properties.

The current state of property data is expensive and inaccurate. Satellite imagery can tell you a roof exists; it cannot tell you the roof is 22 years old with three layers of shingles and a slow leak above the master bedroom. Permit records tell you a renovation was permitted; they do not tell you the quality of the work or whether the contractor was licensed. Actuarial models estimate risk based on zip code and age of construction; they cannot account for the homeowner who replaced every major system in the past five years.

ProLnk, through TrustyPro, captures exactly this data. Every service visit generates a structured record: property address, service type, contractor credentials, photos, job description, estimated cost, and outcome. Aggregated across thousands of properties in a market, this produces a property condition dataset that is more accurate, more current, and more granular than anything currently available.

The monetization model for this data is straightforward: licensing to insurance carriers for underwriting, to mortgage servicers for portfolio risk management, to home warranty companies for claims prediction, and to real estate platforms for buyer intelligence. The data is sold in aggregate, anonymized form — no individual homeowner data is sold without consent.

Verisk generates $3 billion annually selling property data that is largely derived from public records and satellite imagery. ProLnk's data is ground-truth, verified, and current. The premium over existing data sources is significant.

## Part V: The Competitive Moat

The question every investor and partner will ask: why can't Angi, Google, or a well-funded startup replicate this?

The answer is that ProLnk's defensibility comes from three sources that compound over time and cannot be purchased:

**1. The Verified Network**
Building a verified professional network takes time. Each verification requires human review. The 7-checkpoint process cannot be automated without sacrificing accuracy. After 18 months of operation, ProLnk will have a verified network that took 18 months to build. A competitor starting today faces the same 18-month runway — and by then, ProLnk will have 36 months of verified network data.

**2. The Property Data Asset**
Every service visit adds to the property intelligence dataset. This data has no value at 100 properties. It has enormous value at 100,000 properties. The dataset compounds — each new data point makes the existing data more valuable by enabling richer analysis. A competitor cannot buy this dataset. They can only build it, and building it requires the verified professional network, which requires the 18-month runway described above.

**3. The Trust Brand**
Trust is not a feature. It is a brand promise that is earned through consistent execution over time. TrustyPro, as the homeowner-facing brand, is building a trust reputation in the DFW market. That reputation — the recognition that TrustyPro professionals are different from random contractors — is a marketing asset that compounds with every successful job and every satisfied homeowner. It cannot be replicated by a new entrant writing a check.

## Part VI: Answering Your Four Questions

**Q: Where is the product right now — still in concept/prototype, or do you have any working code?**

The platform is beyond prototype. The core infrastructure is built and operational: the partner application and onboarding flow, the 7-checkpoint verification system, the Priority Score engine, the TrustyPro homeowner platform with AI photo analysis, the lead routing system, the commission tracking and payout infrastructure, the admin command center, and the data intelligence layer. The platform has been built to production standards with a full database schema, tRPC API layer, and React frontend. It is not yet in public launch — it is in pre-launch, with the verified partner network being built in DFW ahead of the homeowner-facing launch.

**Q: Who is building it — do you have developers, or are you looking for them?**

The platform has been built using an AI-assisted development approach (AGaaS model). The technical infrastructure exists. The question of ongoing development resources — whether to hire, contract, or continue the AI-assisted model — is a strategic decision that depends on the pace of expansion and the complexity of future features. The current architecture is designed to be maintainable and extensible.

**Q: The core concept is AI-assisted contractor-homeowner matching with routing/dispatch, correct? Anything else I should know about the product vision?**

The routing and matching is correct, but it understates the vision. The full product vision has three horizons:

- **Horizon 1 (now):** Verified professional network + AI-powered homeowner matching + commission-based lead routing in DFW
- **Horizon 2 (12-24 months):** Property intelligence data layer + Home Health Vault + data licensing to insurance/mortgage/real estate buyers
- **Horizon 3 (24-48 months):** National expansion + ProLnk Exchange (commercial real estate) + AGaaS platform where the entire operation runs with minimal human intervention

The patent covers the AI-assisted routing and dispatch methodology, which is the defensible core of the system. The data layer is the long-term value driver. The commission model is the near-term revenue engine.

**Q: Is the patent filed yet, or still in strategy phase?**

Based on the project context, the patent is in strategy/preparation phase. The patent disclosure document has been drafted and is under review. Filing with the USPTO is the next step. The patent covers the AI-assisted contractor-homeowner matching and routing methodology, which is the novel technical contribution of the platform.
